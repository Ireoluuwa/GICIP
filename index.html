Countries & Cities to Visit

Familiarize yourself with the stops along our cultural and innovation tour of Europe.

Berlin

Germany
🌤️ Weather: 15°C - 24°C, Pleasant
💶 Currency: Euro (€), Card Friendly
🎓 Highlights: Bundestag, Tech Hubs, Berlin Wall
💡 Must-Know: Berlin uses extensive public transit. Pack comfortable shoes for 10,000+ daily steps.

Munich

Germany
🌤️ Weather: 14°C - 23°C, Variable rain
💶 Currency: Euro (€), Carry some cash
🎓 Highlights: BMW World, Marienplatz, Innovation Parks
💡 Must-Know: Bavaria is more traditional. Carry some physical cash for small cafes and bakeries.

Amsterdam

Netherlands
🌤️ Weather: 13°C - 21°C, Breezy
💶 Currency: Euro (€), Strictly Card
🎓 Highlights: Canal Cruise, Van Gogh, Tech Incubators
💡 Must-Know: Bicycle pathways are very active. Always watch where you walk to avoid bike collisions!

Paris

France
🌤️ Weather: 16°C - 25°C, Warm
💶 Currency: Euro (€), Card/Cash hybrid
🎓 Highlights: Station F, Louvre, Eiffel Tower, Networking
💡 Must-Know: Paris has high networking requirements. An elegant semi-formal outfit is required.

Frequently Asked Questions

Key details every GICIP participant needs to know about the preparatory phase.

You require all documents listed in Category A (Participant Documents, e.g., international passport, personal letter of introduction, unofficial transcript, and birth certificate) and Category B (provided by the Program Coordinator, such as flight bookings, health insurance, and school cover letters). Explore the Required Documents checklist section above for a detailed breakdown.
The weather is moderately warm but can shift quickly, especially in Munich and Amsterdam. We recommend layering: bring short-sleeves for warm days, cardigans or jackets for cool evenings, and a reliable waterproof jacket or trench coat. Check the detailed packing tables in either the Female or Male Preparation Guide.
An average daily budget of €35 to €50 covers additional meals, snacks, and minor local transit. Germany is mixed (some cash required), while France and the Netherlands are almost entirely card-only. It is highly recommended to secure an international travel card (like Chipper Cash, Geegpay, or a domiciliary card) before traveling, and carry €100-€200 in physical currency as a backup.
You will submit your biometrics (fingerprints) and have your documents verified. The embassy officer will review your application forms and support letters. They may ask standard questions about your study background, sponsorship, and your intent to return to your home country after the immersion program. Arrive 30 minutes early, dress professionally, and ensure your passport photographs meet standard Schengen specifications.

Airport Look

Relaxed Classic Trench

Comfortable, sleek, and travel-ready. A soft knit matching set paired with an oversized trench coat, comfortable sneakers, and a luxury carry-on. Warm layers are perfect for drafty airplanes and immediate climate adjustments upon arrival.
